Abdominal Muscles

Crunches:
            Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Place your hands behind your head and lift your upper body off the ground,engaging your abdominal muscles.


 

Russian Twists:
            Sit on the floor with your knees bent and feet lifted. Lean back slightly, forming a V-shape with your body. Hold a weight (e.g., medicine ball, plate) and rotate your torso from side to side.


Leg Raises:
             Lie on your back with your hands by your sides. Lift your legs straight up towards the ceiling, then slowly lower them back down without touching the floor.


Plank:
            Get into a push-up position with your forearms on the ground. Keep your body in a straight line from your head to your heels. Hold for as long as you can.
 

Back Muscles

Seated cable row
             Top left: This exercise involves pulling a cable handle towards your chest while seated on a machine. It primarily works the latissimus dorsi (lats), rhomboids, and trapezius muscles.


Bent-over barbell row:
             Top right:This exercise involves bending over at the hips and pulling a barbell towards your chest. It also works the lats, rhomboids, and trapezius, but with a greater emphasis on the lower back muscles.


One-arm cable row:
             Bottom left: This exercise involves pulling a cable handle towards your chest with one arm while seated on a machine. It is a good exercise for isolating the lats on each side.


Face pull:
             Bottom right: This exercise involves pulling a cable handle towards your face at a diagonal angle. It primarily works the rhomboids and trapezius muscles, and can also help improve posture.


Chest Muscles

Chest Dips:
             Top left: - This exercise targets the chest muscles, primarily the pectoralis major. It involves dipping down between two parallel bars, using your body weight as resistance.

Cable Chest Flyes:
             Top right- This exercise also targets the chest muscles, but focuses more on the inner chest. It involves pulling a cable machine handle towards the center of your chest, simulating a flying motion.


Incline Dumbbell Press:
             Bottom left: - This exercise works the upper chest muscles It involves lying on an incline bench and pressing dumbbells upwards.


Smith Machine Incline Bench Press:
             Bottom right: - Similar to the incline dumbbell press, this exercise targets the upper chest muscles. However, it uses a Smith machine, which provides a guided path for the barbell, making it easier to maintain proper form.


Shoulders

Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press:
             Top left :This exercise involves sitting on a bench and pressing dumbbells overhead. It primarily works the anterior deltoids (front shoulders).


Machine Shoulder Press:
             Top right:This exercise uses a machine to press weights overhead. It also works the anterior deltoids, but may provide more stability and support.


Standing Dumbbell Shoulder Press:
             Bottom left:This exercise is similar to the seated version, but performed standing. It can help engage the core muscles more.


Lateral Raise:
             Bottom right:This exercise involves raising your arms out to the sides, primarily working the lateral deltoids (side shoulders).


biceps/triceps

Dumbbell Hammer Curls:
             Top left :This exercise involves curling dumbbells with your palms facing each other. It primarily works the brachialis, a muscle on the inner side of the biceps.


Bicep Curls:
             Top right:This exercise involves curling dumbbells with your palms facing forward. It primarily works the biceps brachii, the main muscle of the biceps.


Lying Dumbbell Extensions:
             Bottom left:This exercise involves lying on your back and extending your arms holding dumbbells overhead. It works the triceps, the muscles on the back of your upper arm. It can help engage the core muscles more.


Skull Crushers:
             Bottom right:This exercise involves sitting on a bench and lowering a barbell behind your head. It also works the triceps.


Legs

Seated Leg Curl:
             Top left :This exercise involves sitting on a machine and curling a padded lever upwards. It primarily works the hamstrings, the muscles on the back of your thighs.


Lying Leg Curl :
             Top right:TThis exercise is similar to the seated leg curl, but performed lying on your stomach. It also works the hamstrings.


Popliteal Hamstring Curl:
             Bottom left:his exercise involves lying on your stomach and curling a padded lever upwards with your legs bent at the knees. It targets the lower hamstrings.It can help engage the core muscles more.


Leg Press
             Bottom right:This exercise involves pushing a weighted platform away from you with your legs. It works the quadriceps, the muscles on the front of your thighs.
